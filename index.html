Welcome to My Entrepreneurial Journey

Transforming Reading Culture in Rural Rwanda

Hello! I'm Allan Philip, and I'm passionate about transforming how children in rural Rwanda experience reading. Through my ENT 110 project, I've embarked on an incredible journey to address a critical challenge that I believe holds the key to unlocking Rwanda's future potential.
I discovered that children in rural Rwanda have limited access to engaging books outside their formal education, and reading for personal development is often viewed as non-essential. This realization sparked my mission to create something extraordinary: Book Buddies Lounge - community reading spaces that transform reading from a neglected activity into an exciting, culturally-valued experience.
My Mission: Give every child aged 6-14 the opportunity to explore storybooks and non-fiction through fun, social activities that build the critical thinking and creativity skills essential for Rwanda's future.
My vision is simple yet powerful: This isn't just about books - it's about nurturing the next generation of innovators and leaders. Join me as I share how I identified this problem, developed my solution, and learned invaluable lessons about entrepreneurship, community engagement, and the power of reading to change lives.

How I Discovered a Hidden Crisis

My path from observation to action

My entrepreneurial journey began during a community visit to a rural village in Rwanda. I was volunteering at a local school when I noticed something that would change my perspective forever. During break time, while children in urban areas might be reading picture books or discussing stories, these brilliant young minds were playing with sticks and stones - not because they preferred it, but because it was their only option.
"Books are good, but will they help my granddaughter feed her family?"
- Community Grandmother
I started asking questions. I spoke with teachers, parents, and community leaders. What I discovered broke my heart but also ignited my entrepreneurial spirit. Parents genuinely wanted their children to succeed, but many viewed reading storybooks as "luxury" or "foreign influence." Teachers were overwhelmed, focusing solely on curriculum requirements.
The turning point came when I met 8-year-old Grace, who told me she loved the one fairy tale book at her school but had read it seventeen times because it was the only story available. Her eyes lit up when she talked about imagining new adventures, but then dimmed when she realized she had no new stories to discover.

Introducing Book Buddies Lounge

Community reading spaces that transform culture

My solution is Book Buddies Lounge - welcoming community reading spaces specifically designed for rural Rwanda, where children aged 6-14 can access diverse storybooks and non-fiction through engaging, social reading activities.
Key Features:
  • Social Reading Activities: Peer reading challenges and storytelling sessions
  • Cultural Integration: Local languages and Rwandan values
  • Skills Development: Critical thinking and creativity focus
  • Community Ownership: Local volunteers and parent involvement
The Physical Space: I envision cozy, colorful reading corners with comfortable seating, good lighting, and displays that celebrate both local and international stories. These aren't traditional libraries - they're vibrant community hubs that feel like a friend's living room where amazing stories come alive.
What makes Book Buddies Lounge unique is that it treats reading as a social, celebratory activity rather than solitary homework. We're not just providing books - we're transforming the entire culture around reading in rural communities.

Building and Testing My Vision

From concept to tangible experience

Creating my low-fidelity prototype was both exciting and humbling. I wanted to test whether my concept could actually work before investing time and resources in a full implementation.
My Prototype Approach: I created a physical mockup using cardboard, colored paper, and borrowed furniture to simulate a Book Buddies Lounge corner. I designed activity cards for reading challenges, created sample storytelling prompts, and developed a simple point system for peer engagement.
Testing Process: I set up my prototype at a local community center and invited 12 children aged 6-14 to participate in a 2-hour trial session. I observed how they interacted with the space, which activities engaged them most, and what challenges emerged.
"Can I bring my little sister next time?"
- Samuel, age 9 (during prototype testing)
Key Learning: This simple question made me realize I had been thinking too narrowly about age groups. The most engaged participants were mixed-age groups where older children naturally mentored younger ones.
Iterations I Made:
  • Simplified the badge system based on user feedback
  • Added mobile seating options for flexible group sizes
  • Created spaces for children to display their own artwork and stories
  • Strengthened connections to home for family engagement

What Entrepreneurship Taught Me

Lessons learned and insights gained

This project transformed my understanding of entrepreneurship, problem-solving, and community engagement in ways I never expected.
My Biggest Surprise: I initially thought the main barrier was lack of books. Through my research and interviews, I discovered the real challenge was cultural perception and community systems. Parents weren't against reading - they were protecting their children from what they perceived as irrelevant or potentially harmful influences.
What I'd Do Differently: I would start with community conversations before developing any solution. I spent weeks perfecting my prototype before really understanding what the community wanted. Next time, I'd involve community members as co-designers from day one, not just feedback providers at the end.
Skills I Developed: This project pushed me far outside my comfort zone. I learned to conduct respectful interviews across language and cultural barriers, to iterate based on user feedback rather than my assumptions, and to present ideas in ways that connect with different audiences.
The Validation That Mattered Most: When parents started asking when Book Buddies Lounge would open permanently, I knew we had created something valuable. But the real validation came when children began creating their own stories inspired by what they read - exactly the creativity and critical thinking outcomes I hoped to achieve.

What's Next for Book Buddies Lounge

Building sustainable impact for Rwanda's future

I'm energized by what we've accomplished so far, but this is just the beginning. My next steps focus on sustainable growth, community ownership, and measurable impact.
Immediate Next Steps (Next 3 Months): I'm planning to establish our first permanent Book Buddies Lounge location in partnership with a rural community center that has already expressed interest. This will serve as our proof-of-concept location where we can refine operations and document impact.
Long-term Vision (3-5 Years): I envision a network of community-owned Book Buddies Lounges across rural Rwanda, each adapted to local culture but sharing resources, training, and success stories. More importantly, I want to see measurable improvements in children's creativity, critical thinking, and communication skills.
